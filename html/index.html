Wellstarter

Software engineer, 2018-

Trondheim, Norway


Wellstarter is an oil and gas service company developing a system for improving the observability of inflow rates within oil wells. This is done by generating signals in the form of heat pulses in oil wells which are propagated and affected by the inflow rates upstream and downstream of the source location which can be measured at the "heel" of the well.


I started working part-time at Wellstarter to develop an ERP solution. Being an oil and gas service company, Wellstarter is expected to provide detailed information on every part of its product such as material certificates, logistics tracking, production process and testing. I was given free reigns in approaching this which ment complete freedom in how the end product should appear and what tools to use. You can read more about this project in the projects section.


During my final year at NTNU i also cooperated with Wellstarter on both my project and master thesis. Sjur Mo, Senior Staff Production Engineer and Ph.D in applied physics, supervised and offered great insight into numerically solving physical systems.

I started building a system at a family cabin in 2017 with a hardware stack constisting of a raspberry pi, a z-wave stick and a various z-wave compatible sensors. I set up homeassistant using Docker and published the site using Nginx. In the years since the system has grown to encompass 2 cabins and my family home with the list of hardware now being:

  • 2 Asus barebone machines
  • 2 UPS'es
  • 7 raspberry pi's
  • 5 pi cameras
  • 7 multisensors (temperature, PIR, light, pressure, humidity)
  • 8 smoke detectors, 5 with temperature sensor
  • 6 wall plugs
  • 12 thermostats
The 5 pi's running cameras either livestream video or capture image/video on request. One of the cabins depend on gms/4g making livestreaming a bit costly datawise. The 3 homes each have a dedicated server with the 2 cabins having UPS'es and using Asus machines rather than pi's. This is due to the weakness in the sd-card, causing the pi's to suddenly crash after a few years, always when noone are present. The software stack for the servers at each home are:
  • Docker
  • Homeassistant
  • PostgreSQL
  • Nginx (except for the cabin on mobile net)
