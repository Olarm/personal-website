Wellstarter

Software engineer, 2018-2021

Trondheim, Norway


Wellstarter is an oil and gas service company developing a system for improving the observability of inflow rates within oil wells. This is done by generating signals in the form of heat pulses in oil wells which are propagated and affected by the inflow rates upstream and downstream of the source location which can be measured at the "heel" of the well.


I started working part-time at Wellstarter to develop an ERP solution (think SAP just not Slow And Painful). Being an oil and gas service company, Wellstarter is expected to provide detailed information on every part of its product such as material certificates, logistics tracking, production process and testing. I was given free reigns in approaching this which ment complete freedom in how the end product should appear and what tools to use. You can read more about this project in the projects section.


During my final year at NTNU i also cooperated with Wellstarter on both my project and master thesis. Sjur Mo, Senior Staff Production Engineer and Ph.D in applied physics, supervised and offered great insight into numerically solving physical systems.
